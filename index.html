Real Estate Development

Strategic Position. Innovative Execution.

We are a forward thinking real estate developer, owner, and investor currently operating under a start up phase business model. In all of our real estate development projects we aim to develop lasting relationships with our partners and create financial and societal value for the project shareholders and surrounding community. We develop new construction projects (urban residential and single family) as well as procure and manage rental-income-properties in the Greater Boston Area. Currently, we are undertaking a 19-unit residential development expected to break ground for construction in Summer 2021.

Architecture & Engineering

Design Excellence. Timeless Execution.

We perform the design for our projects in house and offer full engineering (MEP/FP, Structural, Civil, Landscape Architecture, and Code Consulting) services through thoughtful partnerships with a highly qualified pool of consultant partners. As both the Architect of Record and Equity Partner on our projects, and with no competing interests, we can push the bar higher to execute the highest-level design while meeting financial objectives. Each team member takes pride in the exceptional standards of design and ingenuity evident of their projects, and in the timeless quality that shines through every detail.

    StreetBlock Development, LLC is a multidisciplinary real estate development and design firm. Our focus is on multi-family housing projects that elevates the expectations of our strategic partners, investors, and the communities in which we build.

    Our founding partner, Gregory Zurlo, grew up in a densely developed neighborhood in Brooklyn, New York. Calling upon his early childhood experiences observing the physical characteristics of what creates space, the street, and the block he lived on, offered him an opportunity to observe and learn. While a city block is a space for building structures (homes, offices, parks, etc) within the street pattern of a city, Greg saw the block he lived on as a boy a place of identity and character. In his mind his block had both a singular identity and place in the larger system of the City. The street and the block are the spaces we inhabit; a place where public and private meet or overlap.
